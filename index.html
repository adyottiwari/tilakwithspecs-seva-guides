Raag Guide

Ashtayam Seva — The Eight Daily Darshans of Shri Thakur Ji

अष्टयाम सेवा में राग विधान — पुष्टिमार्गीय परम्परा

In Pushtimarg, the seva of Shri Thakur Ji is not merely ritual — it is a living, breathing act of love. The day is divided into eight prahars (Ashtayam), and at each prahar, specific raags are sung that align with the time of day, the season, and the bhav (emotional essence) of that divine moment.

राग वह माध्यम है जिसके द्वारा हम प्रभु को आनन्दित करते हैं। जो राग जिस समय गाया जाता है, वह उस समय की भावना को प्रकट करता है।

This is a guide to the primary raags of each darshan — rooted in the Drupad Dhamar tradition preserved by Shri Gusaiji and the eight Ashta Chaap poet-bhaktas. Every note is an offering. Every raag, a doorway into the lila of Prabhu.

The Full Day at a Glance

Darshan by Darshan

Primary Raags — In Depth

01 · First Darshan

Mangala Darshan

मंगला — प्रभु का प्रथम निद्रा से जगना

~4:30 – 6:00 AM

The day begins in the deep stillness before dawn. Shri Thakur Ji is awakened gently — conch shells are blown, and the kirtankaar sings raags that carry the softness of the predawn hour. The bhav is of tender waking, of a mother calling her beloved child from sleep. This is the most serene and spiritually potent darshan.

"सांझ के सांचे बोल तिहारे" — प्रभु, रात भर कहाँ रहे? अब तो जागिए।

Primary Raags of Mangala
Raag Bhairav Raag Lalit Raag Bibhas
Raag Bhairav The classic dawn raag — solemn, devotional, deeply peaceful. Komal Rishabh and Dhaivat give it a quality of reverence and surrender.
Raag Lalit Sung in the hours just before sunrise. Its unusual structure — with both Madhyams — creates a tender, longing quality, as if calling out to Prabhu.
Raag Bibhas A pre-dawn raag known for its austerity and purity. Its komal swaras evoke the quiet of deep morning — a raag of stillness and surrender.
Kirtan Example from Tradition

Shri Kumbhandasji's first kirtan before Shreenathji was sung at dawn in Bilaval Raag aur Dhammar Taal — a moment that gave birth to the entire kirtan tradition of Pushtimarg. Today, the mangala darshan carries that same energy of divine first-light.

02 · Second Darshan

Shringar Darshan

श्रृंगार — वस्त्र, आभूषण, श्रृंगार की सेवा

~7:00 – 8:00 AM

After being bathed and awakened, Shri Thakur Ji is adorned with seasonal clothes and jewellery. The kirtankaars sing as the mukhiyaji offers each ornament. The raags of shringar are joyful and bright — reflecting the happiness of dressing the beloved. Seasonal attire governs the shringar: pearls in summer, precious stones in winter, gold in spring.

Primary Raags of Shringar
Raag Sorath Raag Dhanashree Raag Hindol Raag Basant Raag Malhar
Raag Sorath A morning raag with a gentle, uplifting quality. Its movement evokes the beauty of a well-adorned deity, serene and majestic.
Raag Hindol / Basant Sung especially in spring (Vasant) — the swinging, joyful quality of Hindol perfectly suits the festive bhav of Holi and Vasant utsav.
Raag Malhar Sung in monsoon months. The Malhar family of raags — associated with rain and Prabhu's love — holds a very special place in Pushtimarg.
Seasonal Note ऋतु के अनुसार राग बदलता है — यह पुष्टिमार्गीय सेवा की विशेषता है।
03 · Third Darshan

Gwal Darshan

ग्वाल — गोचरण, माखन चोरी की झाँकी

~9:00 – 10:00 AM

Shri Thakur Ji goes out with the cowherd boys — the playful, open-air lila of Nandnandan in the forests of Vraj. The kirtans sung here carry the vatsalya bhav (parental love) and the mischief of makhan chori. The raags are bright, energetic and pastoral — evoking the freshness of a Vraj morning.

Primary Raags of Gwal
Raag Dhanashri Raag Gaud Sarang Raag Vrundavani Sarang Raag Gauri Raag Kanharo
Bhav of this Darshan

The kirtans sung here describe माखन चोरी और वात्सल्य भाव — Prabhu stealing butter, playing in the dust, making Yashoda laugh. Vrundavani Sarang is particularly associated with the forests of Vrindavan and carries that quality of divine play.

04 · Fourth Darshan

Rajbhog Darshan

राजभोग — दोपहर का मुख्य भोग

~11:00 AM – 12 PM

The grandest bhog of the day — an elaborate offering of food to Shri Thakur Ji. The raags here are grand and full-bodied, reflecting the majesty of a royal feast. This is one of the five bhogas: Rajbhog is when Prabhu returns from the forest and is served the midday meal with full royal honours. After bhog, Prabhu rests.

Primary Raags of Rajbhog
Raag Gaud Sarang Raag Vrundavani Sarang Raag Shuddh Sarang Raag Noor Sarang Raag Bilawal Raag Hindol Vasant
Sarang Family of Raags The various Sarang raags — Shuddh, Vrundavani, Gaud, Noor — are specifically associated with midday in Indian classical tradition. Their open, expansive quality reflects the fullness of noon.
Raag Bilawal A bright, auspicious raag. Historically, the very first kirtan of Pushtimarg by Shri Kumbhandasji was in Bilawal — making it sacred in the kirtan lineage.
05 · Fifth Darshan

Utthapan Darshan

उत्थापन — दोपहर के विश्राम के बाद जगना

~3:00 – 4:00 PM

Prabhu is awakened from the afternoon rest. Just as Mangala awakens Shri Thakur Ji in the morning, Utthapan does so in the afternoon. The mood here softens from the grandeur of Rajbhog — Prabhu stirs gently, there is again the scene of gocharan in the afternoon. The raags are reflective and serene.

Primary Raags of Utthapan
Raag Shuddh Sarang Raag Vrundavani Sarang Raag Nat Raag Gauri Raag Bhimpalasi
Note on Bhimpalasi

Raag Bhimpalasi — sung in the late afternoon — carries a quality of gentle longing and contemplative rest. Its komal swaras give it a depth particularly suited to the transitional hours between afternoon and evening. यह राग भावुकता और शांति का अनूठा संगम है।

06 · Sixth Darshan

Bhog Darshan

भोग — सायंकालीन भोग, गोपी-भाव, मुरली

~4:30 – 5:30 PM

An evening bhog is offered — and this darshan holds a particularly beautiful bhav. Kirtans sung here are enriched with Gopidasha (the perspective of the gopis), Murali bhav (the flute), and Gopa bhav. The kirtans here describe the love of Prabhu's devotees as He returns in the evening light of Vraj.

Primary Raags of Bhog
Raag Kanhra (Kanharo) Raag Yaman Raag Gaud Sarang Raag Bhimpalasi
Raag Yaman One of the most beloved raags of the evening — its sharpened Madhyam (teevra Ma) gives it an uplifting, ethereal quality. Associated with festivity and divine grace.
Raag Kanharo This raag carries the name of Prabhu Himself — कान्हा. Deep, rich, and full of pathos. Its use in Bhog darshan holds special emotional significance in the haveli tradition.
07 · Seventh Darshan

Sandhya Aarti

संध्या आरती — माँ यशोदा द्वारा आरती, गोधूलि वेला

~6:30 – 7:00 PM

As the cows return home and dust rises on the Vraj paths at dusk, mother Yashoda herself performs the aarti of Shri Thakur Ji. This is the Godohan Jhanki — the milking of cows. The raags here are deeply moving, suffused with the warmth of homecoming. This is perhaps the most emotionally vivid darshan of the day.

Primary Raags of Sandhya Aarti
Raag Sorath Raag Yaman Raag Hamir Raag Purvi
Raag Purvi A deeply twilight raag — its flat Rishabh and Dhaivat combined with the sharp Madhyam create a bittersweet longing unique to the dusk hours. Very special to this darshan.
Raag Hamir Majestic and expansive. Used in the evening to reflect the grandeur of Prabhu's return — a raag of power and devotional fullness.
The Bhav of Sandhya

गोधूलि की वेला में जब गाएँ घर लौटती हैं, तब माँ यशोदा आरती उतारती हैं। The dust of the cows' hooves in the golden evening light — this is the eternal image of Sandhya in Pushtimarg. Every evening aarti in every haveli recreates this very moment.

08 · Eighth Darshan

Shayan Darshan

शयन — निकुञ्ज में रात्रि विश्राम, संयोग श्रृंगार

~8:30 – 9:30 PM

The final darshan of the day — Shri Thakur Ji is prepared for rest. Dinner is offered. The kirtans here describe the Nikunj bhavana (the bower of Vrindavan) and sanyog shringar — the union of divine love. The raags are deep, nocturnal, and soaked in the intimacy of night. They gently lull Prabhu into rest.

Primary Raags of Shayan
Raag Bihag Raag Kanharo Raag Yaman Raag Hamir Raag Adana Raag Nayaki
Raag Bihag The quintessential late-night raag of devotion — rich, romantic, and full of divine love. Its smooth movement and night-time character make it the most fitting companion for Prabhu's rest.
Raag Adana A late-night raag in the Kanhara family — intense, full of yearning, and deeply evocative. Used in Shayan to express the soul's deep longing for Prabhu.
Raag Nayaki A rare and intimate raag used at this final hour — नायकी का भाव — expressing the devotee as the beloved of Prabhu, in the tradition of madhura bhakti.
Raag Kanharo (again) Appearing in both Bhog and Shayan, Kanharo's deep, night-time quality makes it central to the most intimate moments of the day's seva.
Shayan Bhav

The kirtans of Shayan describe the divine sports of निकुञ्ज — वृंदावन का वह गुप्त कुञ्ज जहाँ प्रभु विराजते हैं। These are not sung for public entertainment — they are whispered, as it were, in Prabhu's ear as He is laid to rest. This is the deepest bhav in all of Pushtimarg seva.

Why does Raag matter in Pushtimarg?

Shri Gusaiji — Shri Vitthalnathji — understood that music is not ornamentation. It is the language of the soul. He wrote: "Raag voh hai jo mann ko prasann kare, aur Prabhu ko aanandita kare."

राग वह है जो मन को प्रसन्न करे और प्रभु को आनन्दित करे — यही पुष्टिमार्गीय हवेली संगीत का मूल सिद्धान्त है।

The Drupad Dhamar tradition of Haveli Sangeet — preserved for over 500 years in the Pushtimarg havelis — is one of India's most sacred musical lineages. Even as Dhrupad nearly vanished in the 18th and 19th centuries, the havelis of Pushtimarg kept it alive. Today, kirtankaars like Shri Vitthaldas Bapodara have renewed this flame for a new generation.

Instruments used: Pakhawaj, Sarangi, Dilruba, Tanpura, Veena, Jhanz, Tal, and Flute — each chosen according to the season and the utsav.
